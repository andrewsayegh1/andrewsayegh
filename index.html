My Story

I didn't have a guide.
You do now.

I grew up in Murrieta, California and started at community college — without a counselor who knew what a fellowship was, or what "competitive applications" actually meant. I figured it out through research, rejection, iteration, and eventually, results.

That process took me to UCLA, through two nationally competitive fellowships, a Rangel Finalist distinction, and internships at CAIR National, TIMEP, and in the U.S. House of Representatives. Today I work as a Case Manager at City Net, navigating complex systems on behalf of people who've been failed by them.

Coaching is how I take everything I learned and compress it for you. No generic templates, no recycled advice — just strategy, honesty, and someone in your corner who's been exactly where you're standing.

FAQ

Questions I hear
most often.

Who is this coaching for?
First-gen students, transfer applicants, undergrads pursuing fellowships, recent graduates pivoting to policy or law, and anyone navigating a competitive process without a built-in support system. If that's you, this is for you.
I'm on a tight budget. Can I still work with you?
Yes. A sliding scale is available for students who need it — just mention it when you reach out. The people who most need this coaching shouldn't be priced out of it.
How is this different from generic college counseling?
Most college counselors haven't applied to fellowships, navigated a policy career, or transferred from community college. I have. My coaching is based on what I actually did — not a textbook formula.
What fellowships can you help me with?
Fulbright, Rangel, Boren, Gilman, FLAS, Critical Language Scholarship, and most other nationally competitive fellowships. If you're targeting a specific one, ask — I'll tell you honestly what I can offer.
How quickly can you turn around feedback?
Typically within 48–72 hours. If you're on a deadline, let me know upfront and I'll do my best to prioritize you. Rush turnarounds are available in most cases.
Do you only work with Arab American or MENA students?
Not at all. I work with first-gen students and career-changers from all backgrounds. My lived experience informs my coaching approach — it doesn't limit who I help.
